Getting Started with the Carnivore Diet

Written by Jake M. on April 29, 2025

We like to think of the Carnivore Diet as a journey, not a destination. It's about finding what works best for you and your body. If you just dive in to full-blown carnivore without "phasing in," you're likely to have a bad time. That's why we recommend following these 4 phases:

Phase 1: Prep the Body (1-2 Weeks)

Goal: Reduce carbohydrate dependance slowly.

  • Reduce carbohydrate intake to 50-100g per day - Count them!
  • Increase healthy fats in your diet. Butter, tallow, ghee, etc. - these are your friends! You need to stimulate bile production.
  • Focus on whole, unprocessed foods.
  • Stay hydrated and consider electrolyte supplementation. (2g of sodium minimum)
  • No seed oils. Ever.
  • No refined sugar or sugar-containing drinks.
  • Dairy as tolerated and within your carb threshold.
  • Honey - within your carb threshold.
  • Berries - within your carb threshold.

Phase 2: Keto-Adaptation (2-4 Weeks)

Goal: Become fat-adapted.

  • Reduce carbohydrate intake to 20-30g per day, mostly from incidental sources (eggs, liver, dairy if tolerated).
  • Meals = 70-80% fat by calories, 20-30% protein.
  • Prioritize red meat (beef, lamb) and fatty cuts. Ground beef, untrimmed Tri-tip, etc.
  • Introduce bone broth daily for minerals OR use KetoChow dailly minerals (no sponsor).

Combatting "Keto Flu"

  • Increase unrefined salt intake to 4-5g per day while your body dumps water smuggled by glucose into cells.
  • Increase potassium intake to 2-4g per day.
  • Increase magnesium intake to 300-500mg per day.
  • Salt your food aggressively! (Unrefined, like Redmonds Real Salt)
  • Stay hydrated and consider electrolyte supplementation. We like Re-Lyte from Redmond or Dr. Bergs.

Phase 3: Carnivore (4-6 Weeks)

Goal: Become fully carnivore while fine-tuning digestion.

  • Cut ALL plants now - seasonings without sugar are fine
  • Focus on fatty cuts of meat, organ meats, and bone marrow.
  • Keep meals simple: avoid "carnivore junk food" and Keto treats - they are everywhere and cost $$$.
  • Expect some minor digestion changes: looser stools are normal initially as bile production ramps up.
  • Early on, eat more ground beef - it is a very easy way to balance protein and fat.
  • Limit rendered fat (don't flood with butter/tallow at first - get your fat from the meat you eat).
  • Gradually increase fat over 1-2 weeks.

Phase 4: Welcome to Carnivore, The Proper Human Diet.

This journey isn’t about perfection—it's about progress. If you take the time to transition intentionally through each phase, you’ll set yourself up for long-term success on the carnivore diet and unlock a new level of health and vitality.
